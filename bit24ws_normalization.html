Normalization necessary?¶

For microbial NGS sequencing datasets, compositionality of the data must be dealt with in some manner.

  1. Gloor, G. B., Macklaim, J. M., Pawlowsky-Glahn, V. & Egozcue, J. J. Microbiome datasets are compositional: and this is not optional. Front. Microbiol. 8, 2224 (2017) (https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2017.02224/full)
  2. Lin H, Peddada SD. Analysis of microbial compositions: A review of normalization and differential abundance analysis. NPJ Biofilms Microbiomes. 2020;6:1–13. doi: 10.1038/s41522-020-00160-w (https://www.nature.com/articles/s41522-020-00160-w)
  3. (Bonus after 2) Nearing, J.T., Douglas, G.M., Hayes, M.G. et al. Microbiome differential abundance methods produce different results across 38 datasets. Nat Commun 13, 342 (2022). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-28034-z

Multiple methods exist. For our purposes of further analysis of discretized data, plain normalization by total abbundance is sufficient.
